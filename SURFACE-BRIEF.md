# Surface Brief — Collab Directory LP

*One-page snapshot of the page strategy, audience, and approach.*

## The Page
Landing page for Collabs in Sync early-access signup.
Goal: Convert cold visitors into early collaborator signups.

## The Buyer
**Who:** Skilled songwriter, producer, or vocalist (any of the three)
**Pain:** Tried paying for collaboration, too expensive. Currently hunting on Reddit/DMs.
**Emotional state:** Frustrated, anxious, stuck.
**What they want:** Co-creators willing to bet on shared profit, not hired talent.
**Where they find us:** Reddit, Instagram

## The Promise
"The Platform Built for Profit-Sharing Music Creators"

## Why It Works (Mechanism)
No hunting on Reddit. No expensive hiring. Just match with collaborators who need exactly what you offer.

## The Move
- Claim the niche (profit-sharing focus, not gig work)
- Show the platform works (directory mockup)
- Lower friction (early access, not full signup)
- Build the community before launch

## Awareness Level
**Low-to-Middle.** They know the pain but don't know dedicated solutions exist. Cold traffic from social.

## Page Structure
Full 10-part structure (above + below fold)

## Success Metric
Signups to early-access list that lead to active collaborators on launch.

---

## Quality Bar

### What This Page Must Prove Above the Fold
1. This is specifically for profit-sharing collaborators, not paid gig work (not Fiverr/Upwork)
2. Real musicians can find each other based on what they offer AND what they need
3. The matching system actually works better than Reddit hunting

### What Would Make It Feel Generic
- Generic copy like "Find musicians online" or "Collaborate with talented creators"
- Stock photos of happy musicians smiling
- Vague features without specifics ("Connect with professionals")
- Testimonials that sound made-up (all praise, no real friction)
- Any positioning that could apply to 10 other music platforms

### What Would Make It Feel Trustworthy
- Real profile examples showing actual matching criteria (what they offer, what they need, location, work style, demos)
- Clear explanation: "This is how we match you"
- Acknowledgment of the actual problem: "Reddit hunting takes forever. Hiring is expensive. Here's the third way."
- Transparency about what profit-sharing actually means (you negotiate splits, we don't take a cut)
- No invented numbers, fake testimonials, or stock imagery

### The One Action This Page Must Cause
**Get early access.** (Join the beta, build the community before launch.)

Nothing else. No secondary CTAs, no "explore features," no newsletter signup fighting for attention.

### What Proof Must Appear High on the Page
Since you're pre-launch (no user testimonials yet):
- **Directory mockup/example** showing how profiles actually look (what you offer, what you need, location, work style, demo links)
- **How it's different from Reddit:** Direct comparison of the friction you solve (specific, not vague)
- **Trust signal:** Your background/why you built this (if relevant), or a clear statement of what profit-sharing means on this platform

Do NOT invent:
- User counts ("1,000+ musicians already...")
- Success stories or testimonials
- Time savings claims you can't prove
- Examples of successful collabs (you don't have them yet)

### Mobile Rules
- Profile cards stack vertically and remain readable
- CTA button is thumb-accessible (bottom half of viewport, adequate padding)
- Directory preview is scrollable horizontally or swipeable to show multiple profiles
- All text is readable without pinch-zoom
- Forms (if used) have large touch targets

### Three Mistakes to Avoid
1. **Making it feel like a generic gig marketplace.** If the page could describe Fiverr or Upwork, it's too generic. Emphasize profit-sharing, equity, co-creation — not hired work.
2. **Inventing proof or testimonials.** No made-up user counts, fake quotes, or "success stories." Show the mockup, explain the system, ask for early access. That's honest.
3. **Being vague about collaboration.** Don't say "find musicians." Say "match with songwriters who need producers, producers who need vocalists, vocalists who need beats — and negotiate profit splits directly." Specificity builds trust.

## SECTION ORDER

1. **Hero** — Title + Subtitle + Directory Mockup + Founder Story (social proof) + Join the Beta CTA
2. **Features & Objections** — What you get, why it works, addressing "how do I know they're real?"
3. **Why Profit-Sharing Wins** — The difference between hiring ($300-$1000 per song) vs. equity (mutual investment)
4. **FAQ** — Platform fee model, splits negotiation, contracts, ownership, AI policy
5. **Second CTA** — "Stop hunting on Reddit. Start matching." → "Join the Beta"
6. **Founder's Note** — 4-beat story: shoes → problem → ownership → happy ending

## DESIGN DIRECTION: "Clean Energy" (Minimalist Professional + Mobile-First)

**Visual Thesis:** Bold, focused design with the directory mockup as the star. Blue and orange are intentional; orange only appears on CTAs and key highlights. Black text on white is the foundation. Generous whitespace. Mobile-first: scales beautifully from 320px to desktop. No responsive tricks—everything stacks vertically on mobile, expands on desktop.

**Typography:**
- Display: Bold, geometric sans-serif (DM Sans Bold or Sohne Bold) — confidence without pretension
- Body: Clean, readable serif or high-legibility sans (Lora or Basier Circle) — approachable professionalism
- Hierarchy: 3 sizes only (headline, subhead, body). No medium weights. Clear contrast at every size.

**Color Roles:**
- Blue (#0066ff): Section backgrounds, directory mockup accent border, text weight
- Orange (#ff6b35): CTA buttons ONLY. One button per section. Makes action pop.
- Black (#000000): All body text, strong contrast
- White: Breathing room, negative space, card backgrounds

**Layout Rhythm (Mobile-First):**
- Mobile (320px–767px): Single column, full width, generous padding (16px sides)
- Tablet (768px–1023px): 2 columns with centered content area
- Desktop (1024px+): 3 columns, 60-char max text width, content sits in center (leaves outer air)
- Spacing: 40px vertical gaps on mobile, 60px–80px on desktop
- Grid: 12-column grid, but content constrained to center (creates intentional negative space)
- One idea per section—no information clustering

**Background Elements:**
- Ghosted grey DAW (Digital Audio Workstation) visual in one section (10–15% opacity, behind text)
- Sheet music pattern in another section (same opacity, thematic)
- No other decoration; these are thematic anchors only

**Imagery Strategy:**
- Directory mockup: THE visual (takes up 60% of above-fold width on desktop, 100% on mobile)
- Founder photo: Placeholder (will replace with candid shot), positioned centered above founder note
- Profile avatars: Black & white placeholder photos (80×80px, circular crop)
- No decorative imagery; every image serves a purpose
- High contrast: Black profiles on white cards, blue accents pop against white background

## COPY CONSTRAINTS

- **Above fold:** Keep to ~80–100 words total (title + subtitle only; founder story is visual/image-based)
- **Section heads:** 1–2 lines, punchy, no jargon
- **Body copy:** 2–3 sentences per section max (respect glanceability)
- **CTAs:** Action verbs only ("Join the Beta", "Get Early Access") — no generic "Learn More" or "Submit"
- **No invented proof:** No user counts, success stories, or fake testimonials
- **Founder note:** Exactly 4 beats (shoes → problem → ownership → happy ending), ~150 words total

- **Responsive breakpoints:** 320px (mobile), 768px (tablet), 1024px (desktop)
- **Touch-friendly CTAs:** Buttons 48px × 48px minimum, 16px padding around interactive elements
- **Text legibility:** No smaller than 16px body text; 24px+ for headlines
- **Viewport optimization:** No horizontal scrolling; all content fits within viewport width
- **Form fields (if used):** Large, single-column layout; 40px+ input height
- **Asymmetric elements on mobile:** Stack/center; asymmetry is desktop-only
- **Images on mobile:** Responsive images that scale; mockup may need to be displayed in a carousel or step-by-step format instead of rotating
