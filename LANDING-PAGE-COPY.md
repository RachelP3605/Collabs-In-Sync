**Awareness level:** Low-to-middle. They know they need co-creators willing to bet on shared profit, not hired talent. But they're currently using Reddit/DMs, which are chaotic and slow.

## THE OFFER

**What we provide:** A directory platform where you can find and connect with songwriters, producers, and vocalists who want to collaborate on music for sync licensing.

**Free tier:** Sign up and request intros to 3 potential collaborators.

**Paid tier:** $9/month (annual) or $15/month (monthly) for 20 outreaches per month.

**What we don't do:** We don't handle profit splits or negotiate terms. You and your collaborators agree on what's fair based on the work each person does. (We provide education on best practices and common standards.)

### Slot 7: Why Profit-Sharing Wins

**Hiring a session singer or producer costs $300–$1,000+ per song.**

When you pay upfront, they deliver the work. Then you move on.

**Profit-sharing is different.**

When someone's incentivized by the back-end, they care about the *quality* of what you create together. They're betting on the same outcome as you: that the song is good enough to sync.

This changes everything:
- They suggest improvements (because it's their name on the song too)
- They're invested in the timeline (they want to get paid, so they finish on schedule)
- They match your energy (they're excited about the *song*, not just the paycheck)

**You get quality collaborators who actually care.**

They're not phoning it in. They're building a catalog with you. If the song gets licensed, you both win. If it doesn't, you both learned something and move to the next one.

**For sync licensing, this is exactly what you need.**

You need people who understand that quality matters—because quality is what gets placed. Profit-sharing attracts people who get that. Hiring attracts people who are just getting paid for time.

### Slot 8: FAQ

**Q: How does the platform make money if you don't take a cut?**
A: Paid members get more outreaches per month ($9 or $15/month, depending on billing). Free members get 3 intros to test it out first.

**Q: What if we can't agree on splits?**
A: You negotiate directly with your collaborators. We provide best-practice guides on what's standard in the industry (50/50 if both did equal work, adjusted based on actual contributions, etc.). But the agreement is between you.

**Q: Do I need a contract?**
A: Yes. We recommend written agreements between collaborators. (We'll link to resources on how to create them, but you handle that part.)

**Q: Who owns the music we create together?**
A: You decide that upfront. We recommend: each collaborator owns a percentage of the master and composition based on what they contributed. Talk to a music lawyer if you're unsure.

**Q: Can I use AI to create parts I'm missing?**
A: AI is not accepted in most sync licensing deals. If you want to license on major platforms (Spotify's sync library, music supervisors, etc.), you'll need human talent. That's why you're here.

**Q: How do I know someone is serious about this?**
A: Profiles with demos carry weight. People who post samples of their work are serious. Start conversations with people who have verified demos and clear about what they're looking for.
